Iron Stand

🧺 **What's an Iron Stand?**

Basically, it's what you put your clothes on when you iron, so you don't burn your table! It's gotta be steady, not melt, and be the right height so your back doesn't ache.

🧩 **What it's Made Of:**

*   **Top Part:** This is where the magic happens. It's flat so the heat spreads evenly. Usually, it has a cover and some cushiony stuff underneath. Plus, it has tiny holes to let the steam out so things don't get soggy.

*   **Legs:** Usually metal (like steel or aluminum). They fold up, which is great for hiding it away. You can usually make them taller or shorter.

*   **Cushion &amp; Cover:** The cushion is usually felt, foam, or cotton. The cover has to stand the heat and not stick to your clothes.

*   **Iron Spot:** A safe place to park your hot iron when you aren't using it.

*   **Bonus Stuff:** Some have a place to hang clothes, a mini-board for sleeves, or a spot for your steam machine.

🧱 **What Things Are Made Of:**

| Part      | Stuff It's Made Of | Why       |
| :-------- | :----------------- | :-------- |
| Legs      | Steel, Aluminum     | Strong      |
| Top       | Metal or Wood       | For Steam |
| Cushion   | Cotton, felt, foam  | makes it Smooth |
| Top Cover | Cotton, Teflon      | Heat Proof   |

⚙️ **Kinds of Iron Stands**

*   **Simple Foldable:** Light and easy to stash. Good if you don't have much room.
*   **Adjustable Height:** You can change the height.
*   **Wall Hugger:** Sticks to the wall. Folds up out of the way.
*   **Hidden:** Inside a cabinet.
*   **Table Top:** Small, for trips or small quick jobs.
*   **For Steam Irons:** Stronger and catches water.

🧰 **How to Use It Right**

*   **Get Ready:** Pop out the legs and lock them tight. Set it up so its at your elbow height.
*   **While Ironing:** Lay your clothes out flat.
*   **All Done:** Let your iron cool before you put the stand away.

🔥 **Be Safe!**

*   Flat surface only!
*   Cord shouldn't be a tripping hazard.
*   Don't leave a hot iron alone!
*   Keep it away from water and curtains.
*   Make sure the cover can take the heat.

🧽 **Keep it Clean**

| Part     | How to Clean   | How Often    |
| :------- | :------------- | :----------- |
| Cover    | Wipe it        | Once a Month |
| Cushion  | When it's Flat | When Needed  |
| Legs     | Wipe it        | Once a Month |
| Iron Spot | Clean Gunk     | Often        |

**Tip:** Get a new cover if yours is bumpy.

📏 **Picking the Right One**

Things to think about:

*   Size: Big enough for shirts and pants.
*   Height: Can you change it?
*   Weight: Easy to move, but steady?
*   Cushion: Thick is better.
*   Where will you put it?
*   Does steam go through it okay?

🧠 **Good Ideas**

*   Cotton covers last.
*   Change the cushion every year or two.
*   Low heat for fancy clothes.
*   Use a cloth between the iron and clothes to be extra safe.
*   Store it standing up.
